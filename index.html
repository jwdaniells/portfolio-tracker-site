Questions

Frequently asked

Do I need to be technical to use this?

+

Not at all. If you can have a text conversation, you can use this. The setup is guided — Claude asks you questions and builds the tracker from your answers. No coding, no spreadsheet formulas, no configuration files.

What Claude subscription do I need?

+

You'll need a Claude Pro subscription or above (currently from £18/month at claude.ai). The tracker lives inside a Claude Project, which is a Pro feature. Claude Free does not support Projects. This is separate from the tracker purchase.

Is my financial data safe?

+

Yes. Your tracker lives entirely within your own Claude account. We never see your data — there are no third-party servers, no data synchronisation, no open-banking connections. Everything stays in your Claude Project. You own it completely.

What account types does it support?

+

All standard UK account types: SIPP, Stocks & Shares ISA, Lifetime ISA, General Investment Account, Workplace Pension (including defined benefit), and cash holdings. The retirement planner specifically supports DB pension income streams and State Pension start dates.

How do prices stay up to date?

+

Claude can fetch current fund prices using the ISIN or fund name when you ask it to refresh. You can do a full price update in under a minute by asking Claude to "refresh all prices." The tracker also records historical prices when you update, building up a price history over time.

Can I update it myself if my situation changes?

+

Absolutely. The package includes a library of update prompts for common tasks — adding a fund, changing a contribution, updating a pension start date, and more. The tracker is designed to evolve as your life does. You can also ask Claude to modify any part of it directly.

Is this financial advice?

+

No. This is a modelling and tracking tool. It helps you organise, visualise and project your own financial data — it does not make investment recommendations or constitute regulated financial advice. For personalised advice, please consult a qualified financial adviser.
